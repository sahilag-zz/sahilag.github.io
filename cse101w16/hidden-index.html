(Review:) Homework policy

Homework assignments #1 - #5 will be due on Thursdays of Weeks 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 in lecture during the first five minutes of class. The homework assignments reinforce the subject matter and skills learned in class, and are an integral part of the learning process. Each of the five homework assignments has equal weight. Due to resource limits, homework grading may be sampling-based – i.e., not all problems will be graded in detail.

  • Lateness - Maximum credit on a homework assignment drops to 50% immediately after the deadline, and to 0% after the lecture immediately following the deadline.
  • Rigor and Conciseness - When you are asked to devise an algorithm, unless otherwise specified you must establish correctness and running time. You should strive to be complete yet concise in your answers.

(Review:) Grading Inquiries (including Regrade Requests)

Inquiries about your grade (midterms, quizzes, extra credit assignments) should be directed in writing (email) to the TAs, no more than 7 days after the grade was posted online. All of these grades become final after 7 days. The only exception to this is your homeworks: to give you a longer window for pickup and review, you may request regrading or any correction of score entry errors up to 14 days after the grade was posted online. (For Homework #5, we will try to have scores posted by the final exam, and homeworks available for pickup after the final - but the window for regrade requests can only be until the end of finals week.)

(Review:) Academic Integrity

(Adapted in part from Cynthia Lee Bailey)

  • Plagiarism of homework is a serious breach of academic integrity, and will affect your entire grade for the course, not just the portion allotted to homework.
  • Any collaboration while taking quizzes or in-class exams will be considered a breach of the UCSD Policy on Integrity of Scholarship and will be dealt with as such.
  • Do not cheat. If you are suspected of cheating, we will enforce the UCSD Policy on Integrity of Scholarship. This means that your case will be referred to the Dean of your college, who will determine your guilt or innocence, and who will determine your punishment if guilty. Punishment may be up to and including an F in the course, academic probation or suspension from UCSD.
  • Use of any assistance not explicitly authorized by the instructor is considered cheating. Translation: you cannot assume something is ok just because we haven't said not to do it. If you have any questions about this, please ask.
